ROPA SOLUTION – IN-PRODUCT FAQ CONTENT
1. GENERAL ROPA 
What is a ROPA?
A Record of Processing Activities (ROPA) is a document that outlines how your organization collects, uses, stores, and shares personal data. It is a key requirement under regulations like GDPR and NDPA and helps demonstrate compliance.
Who needs to maintain a ROPA?
Any organization that processes personal data, especially those handling customer, employee, or vendor data, should maintain a ROPA. This includes SMEs, startups, and large enterprises.
Why is a ROPA important?
A ROPA helps you understand your data flows, identify risks, and demonstrate compliance during audits or regulatory reviews.
2. PROCESSING ACTIVITY
What is a processing activity?
A processing activity is any operation performed on personal data, such as collecting customer information, storing employee records, sending marketing emails, or sharing data with third parties.
How do I define a processing activity?
Think of it as a business function. For example:
•	Customer onboarding 
•	Payroll management 
•	Marketing campaigns 
Each of these should be recorded as a separate activity.
3. PURPOSE OF PROCESSING
What does “purpose of processing” mean?
This refers to the reason why personal data is being collected or used.
How do I define the purpose clearly?
Be specific. For example:
•	“To process employee salaries” 
•	“To deliver products to customers”
Avoid vague statements like “for business use.” 
4. LEGAL BASIS
What is legal basis for processing?
Legal basis is the lawful reason your organization is allowed to process personal data under regulations like GDPR.
Which legal basis should I choose?
Common options include:
•	Consent 
•	Contract 
•	Legal obligation 
•	Legitimate interest 
Choose the one that best fits why you are processing the data.
5. DATA SUBJECTS
Who are data subjects?
Data subjects are the individuals whose personal data you process.
Examples of data subjects:
•	Customers 
•	Employees 
•	Vendors 
•	Website visitors 
6. DATA CATEGORIES
What are data categories?
These are the types of personal data you collect.
Examples include:
•	Name, email address, phone number 
•	Financial information 
•	Identification data 
What is sensitive personal data?
Sensitive data includes information like health records, biometric data, or religious beliefs and requires extra protection.
7. DATA RECIPIENTS / THIRD PARTIES
Who are data recipients?
These are individuals or organizations you share personal data with.
Examples include:
•	Payment processors 
•	Cloud storage providers 
•	HR software vendors

 8. DATA RETENTION
What is data retention?
Data retention refers to how long personal data is stored before it is deleted.
How do I determine retention period?
Base it on:
•	Legal requirements 
•	Business needs 
•	Contractual obligations 
Avoid keeping data longer than necessary.
9. INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS
What is a data transfer?
This occurs when personal data is transferred outside your country or region.
When should I include this?
If you use:
•	Cloud providers hosted abroad 
•	International vendors 
10. SECURITY MEASURES
What are technical and organizational measures?
These are controls you put in place to protect personal data.
Examples include:
•	Encryption 
•	Access controls 
•	Employee training 
•	Security policies 
 11. CONTROLLER VS PROCESSOR
What is a data controller?
The organization that decides why and how personal data is processed.
What is a data processor?
A third party that processes data on behalf of the controller.
12. UPDATING YOUR ROPA
How often should I update my ROPA?
Your ROPA should be updated whenever:
•	New processes are introduced 
•	Vendors change 
•	Regulations evolve 
At minimum, review it annually.
13. COMMON MISTAKES
What are common ROPA mistakes?
•	Using vague descriptions 
•	Not updating regularly 
•	Ignoring third-party processors 
•	Not documenting legal basis
